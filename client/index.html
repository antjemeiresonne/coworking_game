    Did you know:

    Use Energy-Efficient Appliances: Choose appliances with the ENERGY STAR label, which are designed to use less energy without sacrificing performance.

    Cook with Lids On: Cover pots and pans while cooking to retain heat, which reduces cooking time and energy usage.

    Utilize Residual Heat: Turn off the oven or stovetop a few minutes before your food is fully cooked. The residual heat will finish the cooking process.

    Defrost in the Refrigerator: Plan ahead and defrost frozen foods in the refrigerator. This reduces cooking time and helps keep your fridge cooler.

    Opt for Smaller Appliances: Use a microwave, toaster oven, or slow cooker instead of the oven for smaller meals. They use less energy and are more efficient for quick or small-scale cooking.

    Keep Your Fridge and Freezer Full: A full fridge and freezer retain cold better than an empty one. If needed, fill empty spaces with water jugs or ice packs.

    Regular Maintenance: Clean the coils of your refrigerator and check door seals to ensure efficient operation. Dusty coils and poor seals make your fridge work harder and consume more energy.

    Match Pot Size to Burner: Use pots and pans that match the size of your burners to ensure that all the heat is used efficiently, rather than being wasted around the sides.

    Use the Dishwasher Efficiently: Run your dishwasher only when it's full and use the energy-saving mode if available. Skip the heated dry option and let dishes air dry.

    Switch to LED Lighting: Replace incandescent bulbs with LED lights, which use significantly less energy and have a longer lifespan.
